His current research interests include federated learning, swarm learning, and Edge AI.

Title: Resource-Aware and Personalized Federated Learning via Clustering Analysis

Abstract

The proliferation of connected edge devices (e.g., smartphones, medical wearables, IoT devices, vehicles, etc.) contributes an abundance of huge amounts of data, which become a valuable resource for improving the performance of Machine Learning (ML) models. To take full advantage of the on-device data, traditional ML requires gathering all separate datasets to a centralized location for training model. However, centralizing data brings prohibitive communication costs, network resource usage, heavy traffic, and privacy concerns. To address the aforementioned challenges, Federated Learning (FL), which is an emerging approach, is used to train a joint model across distributed edge devices while maintaining privacy. Despite its immense potential, FL still requires plenty of significant resources, such as time, computation power, energy, bandwidth availability, etc. More importantly, the computing capabilities available on training devices may vary over time. Furthermore, the devices involved in FL training may have varying training data sets that differ in size and distribution. This can result in unstable and slow convergence of the FL models. These differences can have an impact on the FL process and ultimately lead to suboptimal model performance in a heterogeneous federated network.

In this thesis, we have addressed several of the challenges mentioned above. Initially, an FL algorithm that utilizes cluster analysis to address communication overhead is proposed, which is a major bottleneck for FL, particularly for complex models, large-scale applications, and frequent updates. The subsequent step involved an extension of the aforementioned study to encompass wireless networks (WNs), where an energy-efficient transmission poses a significant challenge due to the limited resources of WSNs. This has motivated us to continue with a comprehensive overview and classification of the advanced developments in context-aware edge-based AI models, focusing on sensor networks. The review also investigated the related challenges and motivations for adopting AI techniques, along with an analysis of existing research gaps. To optimize the aggregation of the FL model and alleviate communication costs, the first study is extended with an FL-based cluster optimization approach. Furthermore, to reduce the detrimental effect of the diverse distribution of data between edge devices, i.e., data heterogeneity on FL, a new study of group-personalized FL models is presented. Finally, inspired by the aforementioned FL models, evaluation methods for a client's contribution by measuring its behavior during training are proposed, without requiring excessive computational resources.

The FL algorithms proposed in this thesis are evaluated on various real-world datasets. This comprehensive evaluation allows us to analyze both the training model and FL performance. The results of extensive experiments showed the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed FL techniques in for improved communication efficiency, resource efficiency, accelerating model convergence, efficient aggregation, and reduced data heterogeneity compared with different state-of-the-art approaches.
